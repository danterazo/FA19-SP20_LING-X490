In 1946 , and the following 18 years , he organized and operated a machine plant for the production and precision machining of automotive parts and engine assemblies and aircraft parts. Subsequently from 1964 through 1973 , Mr. Butler was a marketing analyst for new inventions. In 1968 he became a Senior Manufacturing Engineer for Lockheed Aircraft Co. at their Palmdale , California , plant , where extensive development was under way for the L-1011 aircraft. He resigned from this corporate associateship to devote full energy and time to his greatest and all-consuming desire to serve God and Nation .
