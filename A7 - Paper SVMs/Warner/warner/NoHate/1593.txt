The themes of combat and womanhood you emphasize here do belong together , but it 's challenging to harmonize them adequately. My mention of " war " and " combat " in this essay referred to how a military attitude by a folk citizenry is a prerequisite for any effective opportunities among Aryan men. I was thinking how in Hesiod 's " Works and Days " prosperity is the reward for victory in the Trojan War and how this is the true pattern of life for our men. Hence Kamph precedes prosperity .
